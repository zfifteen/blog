* Introduction  
** Motivation for revisiting TSI in climate models  
** Scope: TSI composition, upper-atmosphere coupling, GEC integration  

* How TSI is composed today  
** Instrumental TSI: space-based radiometers, composites, uncertainties  
** Modeled TSI: SATIRE, NRLTSI, other reconstructions and baselines  
** Spectral vs total: SSI vs TSI, UV/visible/NIR partitioning  

* How current physics describe solar energy  
** Radiative transfer framework from top-of-atmosphere to surface  
** Stratospheric and mesospheric photochemistry (UV-driven processes)  
** Energetic particle precipitation and high-atmosphere energy inputs  

* What is known and not known about solar radiation interaction with the Earth  
** Well-constrained: TSI variability amplitude, 11-year cycle, basic heating patterns  
** Partially constrained: SSI variability, upper-atmosphere dynamics, ozone response  
** Poorly constrained: indirect pathways via clouds, aerosols, circulation shifts  

* How TSI is integrated into climate models today  
** Prescribed TSI/SSI forcings in CMIP-class models (CMIP5, CMIP6, CMIP7 plans)  
** Treatment in models with vs without interactive chemistry  
** Known structural limitations and forcing inconsistencies  

* What is the global electric circuit (GEC)  
** Basic structure: surface–atmosphere–ionosphere current loop  
** Main generators: thunderstorms, electrified clouds, ionospheric sources  
** Key observables: fair-weather electric field, ionospheric potential  

* Knowns and unknowns about the GEC  
** Knowns: large-scale structure, diurnal and seasonal behavior, solar-cycle modulation  
** Unknowns: quantitative impact on clouds, aerosols, and climate trends  
** Open questions: cosmic rays, aerosols, and ion-mediated microphysics links  

* Known and unknowns about the relationship between TSI and the GEC  
** Solar modulation of ionization, conductivity, and global circuit strength  
** Observed correlations vs causal mechanisms  
** Gaps: lack of coupled TSI–GEC–cloud microphysics parameterizations  

* GEC and TSI in climate modeling  
** Current status: standalone GEC models and first couplings into GCMs  
** Missing physics in standard CMIP configurations  
** Conceptual pathways from TSI/SSI to GEC to cloud and circulation changes  

* Computational and modeling challenges  
** Scale separation: resolving microphysics inside global models  
** Data limitations: sparse atmospheric electricity observations  
** Cost/benefit: when added complexity meaningfully changes projections  

* Research directions and proposals  
** Minimal, testable extensions to existing models (TSI/SSI plus simple GEC couplings)  
** Observational campaigns to constrain key parameters  
** Roadmap: from idealized experiments to integrated Earth system models
