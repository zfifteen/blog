# They Found Inflammation. Then They Made It Disappear.

Between 2020 and 2024, an estimated 400 million people worldwide developed Long-COVID, a post-infectious syndrome characterized by fatigue, cognitive impairment, pain, and neurological symptoms that persist long after acute SARS-CoV-2 infection has resolved. Many of these patients were previously healthy. Many remain unable to work, maintain relationships, or perform basic daily tasks years after their initial infection. They are not rare cases. They are one of the largest medically underserved populations in modern history.

The central dispute in Long-COVID medicine is not primarily about symptoms. It is about causation. Two competing frameworks exist: one holds that persistent symptoms reflect ongoing biological pathology, including inflammation, immune dysregulation, viral persistence, and neurological injury; the other holds that symptoms are maintained by psychological mechanisms, including fear, catastrophizing, and learned illness behavior. This is not an abstract academic disagreement. The framework a doctor accepts determines whether a patient receives investigation or therapy, biomedical research or behavioral intervention, legitimacy or dismissal.

This dispute has a history. Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome, a condition that shares substantial clinical overlap with Long-COVID, was classified as psychosomatic for decades. Patients, predominantly women, were told their exhaustion was depression, their pain was anxiety, their cognitive failure was catastrophizing. The treatment offered was cognitive behavioral therapy and graded exercise. We now know, from rigorous biological research, that ME/CFS involves measurable immune abnormalities, mitochondrial dysfunction, and altered gene expression. The psychosomatic classification was not scientifically derived. It was institutionally convenient. It saved insurance companies money, relieved health systems of diagnostic responsibility, and placed the burden of illness back onto the people carrying it.

Long-COVID arrived into this exact institutional environment. Patients organized early, pushed for biological research, and accumulated a significant body of evidence pointing toward inflammatory and neurological mechanisms. Biomarker studies became a flashpoint: if inflammation could be measured in blood, the biological framework would be harder to dismiss. If it could not, the door to psychosomatic reclassification would reopen.

## A study published in *Scientific Reports* in March 2026 walked through that door.

***

Four inflammatory biomarkers, TNF-α (p=0.007), IL-6 (p=0.02), TREM2 (p=0.019), and CRP (p=0.035), were each statistically significant in Long-COVID patients before any correction was applied. These are not noise. TNF-α at p=0.007 with a 95% confidence interval of 0.08-0.42 NPQ units is a finding that warrants investigation, not dismissal.

The researchers then applied a Benjamini-Hochberg False Discovery Rate correction. The paper's Methods section states this explicitly: *"For the NULISAseq results we applied the Benjamini-Hochberg False Discovery Rate (FDR) to correct for multiple testing (n=124)... The n=124 refers to the total number of analytes in the full discovery panel that was run, from which the biomarkers for this study were selected."*

Seven of those 124 analytes are reported in this paper. The statistical penalty for 117 analytes whose results were never published was factored into the correction applied to every signal that was. Under Benjamini-Hochberg at q=0.05 applied to only the 7 reported comparisons, the rank-1 threshold is 0.0071. TNF-α at p=0.007 passes it. The paper's own correction scale is the only mathematical configuration under which TNF-α disappears. After absorbing the weight of those 117 invisible comparisons, TNF-α's FDR-adjusted p-value lands at exactly 0.05, the precise boundary of non-significance. That is not a scientific finding. That is an arithmetic outcome.

After this correction erased all four signals, the paper introduced an alternative framework for understanding persistent Long-COVID symptoms: *"psychological factors such as fear-avoidance beliefs and catastrophic interpretations of bodily sensations can perpetuate symptoms by maintaining a state of autonomic hyperarousal and hindering physical reconditioning."* The citation supporting this claim is not a mechanistic study of Long-COVID pathophysiology. It is a cognitive behavioral therapy trial.

This is the same language used for decades to deny ME/CFS patients biological legitimacy, redirect them toward cognitive behavioral therapy, and foreclose investigation into organic disease mechanisms. Patients who lived through that era will recognize the phrasing immediately. It is not clinical language. It is institutional language. It is the vocabulary of a diagnostic framework designed to end inquiry rather than advance it.

The cohort was 85.4% female. Women with post-infectious conditions have historically borne the heaviest burden of psychosomatic reclassification. This is not coincidental. It is a documented pattern with a documented body count, measured in delayed diagnoses, denied treatments, and decades of patients told their biology was their psychology.

Lead author Henrik Zetterberg discloses advisory, consulting, or lecture sponsorship relationships with more than thirty pharmaceutical and diagnostics firms, including Roche, Biogen, Lilly, Novo Nordisk, Eisai, Merck Sharp and Dohme, Denali, Apellis, Siemens Healthineers, Prothena, Quanterix, and ALZpath, among others, all with direct commercial interests in neurodegeneration biomarker development. The paper whose conclusions concern neuroinflammation biomarkers was co-authored by someone whose financial relationships in the neuroinflammation biomarker space span more than thirty firms. That conflict is disclosed. What it is not is immaterial.

A study that detected real pre-correction inflammation, applied a statistical correction whose scale is explicitly acknowledged to include 117 analytes whose data was withheld from publication, and then directed interpretive attention toward patient psychology as the explanatory framework is not a neutral scientific contribution. It is a study that found something, structured its analysis to erase it, and then told sick people the problem is how they think about their bodies.
